RPI's Campus Is Not Accessible

Many main routes and entryways have stairs, uneven ground, or heavy doors making them inaccessible.

Some buildings don't have elevators and some of the older ones that do are physically difficult to use. These can make large sections of campus completely inaccessible.

Additionally, there is a lack of gender neutral bathrooms on campus, especially accessible ones, with many buildings having none and most being single occupancy and hard to find.

Dear Administration,

Ensuring our campus is accessible is more than a legal obligation-- it is a commitment we make to foster an inclusive and diverse environment where all students, regardless of their abilities, can thrive and contribute. Through improvements in accessibility, we are working towards enhancing student satisfaction, improving retention, and ultimately creating a more welcoming and inclusive environment.

Can YOU see the retention problem?

When the University of Imagine in NY made the decision to improve accessibility on campus, the feedback they received from students was overwhelmingly positive “[…] skyrocketed student retention rates by 20% within the first year" (Dr. Elizabeth Armstrong, Campus Accessibility Director).

If morality isn't loud enough, can YOU see the income opportunity?

As we strive to make these improvements, let us remember the words of Dr. David Mitchell, Director of Disability Services at the University of Global:

"By investing in accessibility, we invest in our students. It's a testament to our commitment to equality, to offering each student a fair and equal opportunity to succeed."

We look forward to YOUR support in this endeavor, as we work together to create a truly inclusive campus environment that students can proudly call home.
